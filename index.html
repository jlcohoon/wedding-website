Event Information

The big event takes place September 2, 2018 at 5:00PM at Panorama Event Barn in Charlottesville, VA. A brief outdoor ceremony will precede the celebration.

Transportation

Free parking will be available. The event site is in a rural area just outside of Charlottesville, so if you are unfamiliar with the area or don't want to arrange a designated driver, we recommend using the shuttle service that will be arranged to take guests back and forth between downtown Charlotesville and the venue. More details about the shuttle service will be posted closer to September 2nd. There is not likely to be good cell service at the event venue, so we do not recommend that you try to use Uber to get a ride home.

Food and Drinks

A family style dinner will be served around 7:00. Snacks will be available before dinner. There will be vegetarian and vegan options, however we ask that you include any dietary restrictions in your RSVP so that we can be sure there's enough for everyone.

Guests can enjoy an open bar serving local wine and some of our favorite beers. Until the dance floor opens, we will also be giving tastings of mezcal, a smoky, agave derived liquor that we've enjoyed since moving to Texas.

Attire

We want you looking and feeling dazzling! Cocktail attire and your favorite dancing shoes will ensure success. The barn has no air conditioning, so leave your heavy jackets at home.

Travel Information

Thank you for making the trip to see us! Below, we've put together some information for you to help you make the most of your Labor Day weekend in Charlottesville. You can also reference this map to see our favorite activities, restaurants, and the pickup/dropoff locations for the event shuttle (shuttle locations will be added closer to the event).

Accomodations

The event will be held a little northwest of Charlottesville, giving you several lodging options. If you would like to stay as close to the event as possible, we recommend The DoubleTree or an AirBnB in Earlysville. Some of the most affordable options can be found on US-29. If you would like to stay closer to the attractions Charlottesville has to offer, we recommend a room in one of the several hotels on West Main Street or at the Omni. These latter options will put you walking distance from from the historic University of Virginia grounds and the Downtown Mall.

The exact shuttle pickup and dropoff location will be determined closer to the event, but it will be within walking distance of hotels on West Main Street.

Activities

Charlottesville is a beautiful place to spend a holiday weekend. If you have the time, we highly recommend that you explore the area. Check out the list below, or see what's near you on this map we've made for the occasion.

Activities in Charlottesville:

  • Dine, shop, or walk on the Downtown Mall
  • Tour the grounds, gardens, and museum at UVA
  • See a show downtown
  • Spend the afternoon at the pool
  • Walk the Saunders-Monticello trail
  • Visit Monticello
  • Eat donuts and see the view at Carter Mountain

Activities in the surrounding area:

  • Hike Humpback Rock
  • Enjoy the scenery on Skyline Drive
  • Hike to and swim in Blue Hole
  • Go to a brewery in Nelson County
  • Visit a vineyard
  • Go tubing on the James River

Airports

Charlottesville has its own small airport (CHO) that travelers from out of town may choose to fly into. There are several other airports that may offer cheaper or more convenient flights, but please note that those alternatives are a non-trivial drive from Charlottesville. The Richmond airport (RIC) is just over an hour east of Charlottesville, while the Dulles (IAD) and Reagan (DCA) airports are about two hours north.
